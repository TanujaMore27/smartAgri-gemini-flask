Guava

Soil Fertility:

  • Adaptability: Guava is quite adaptable and can grow in various soil types.
  • Preferred Soil: Thrives in well-drained, fertile, sandy loam to clay loam soils.
  • pH: A pH range of 6.5 to 7.5 is generally preferred.
  • Drainage: Good drainage is essential, as guava trees are susceptible to waterlogging.

Temperature:

  • Climate: Guava prefers a tropical and subtropical climate.
  • Optimal Temperature: 15°C to 30°C.
  • Tolerance: Can tolerate a range of temperatures, but frost can be damaging.

Guava Varieties in India:

  • Allahabad Safeda: A popular variety known for its white flesh and good flavor.
  • Sardar (Lucknow 49): A high-yielding variety with creamy white flesh.
  • Lalit: A pink-fleshed variety known for its attractive color and flavor.
  • Pant Prabhat: A dwarf variety.
  • Arka Mridula: Also a dwarf variety, known for high-yielding properties.
  • Punjab Pink: Fruit with red flesh.
