Rice

Soil Fertility:

  • Soil Types: Rice can grow in a variety of soils, but it thrives in clayey or clay loam soils that have good water-holding capacity.
  • Puddled soils are ideal for lowland rice cultivation.
  • pH: Rice can tolerate a wide pH range, but it prefers slightly acidic to neutral soils (pH 5.5 to 6.5).
  • Nutrient Requirements: Rice requires essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and micronutrients.
  • Organic matter is crucial for soil fertility and water retention.

Temperature:

  • Climate: Rice is primarily a tropical and subtropical crop that requires warm and humid conditions.
  • Temperature Range: Optimal temperatures for rice growth range from 20°C to 35°C.
  • High temperatures during flowering can negatively impact yield.
  • Water: Rice requires abundant water, especially during the vegetative growth stage.

Rice Varieties in India:

  • Basmati: Known for its long, slender grains and aromatic fragrance. Popular in northern India.
  • Indica: Long or medium-grain rice. Widely grown throughout India.
  • Japonica: Short, sticky grains. Grown in some regions.
