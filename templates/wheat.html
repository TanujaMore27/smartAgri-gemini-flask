Wheat

Soil Fertility:

  • Soil Types: Wheat grows best in well-drained loamy and clay loam soils.
  • pH: A pH range of 6.0 to 7.5 is preferred.
  • Drainage: Good drainage is essential to avoid waterlogging.

Temperature:

  • Climate: Wheat thrives in cool and dry climates.
  • Temperature Range: Optimal temperatures range from 10°C to 25°C.
  • Higher temperatures during grain filling can reduce yield.
  • Cold weather is essential for vernalization in some varieties.

Wheat Varieties in India:

  • HD 2967: A high-yielding variety popular in northern India.
  • PBW 343: A widely cultivated variety in Punjab.
  • Lok 1: Grown extensively in central and western India.
  • DBW 222: Known for disease resistance and good yield.
  • GW 322: A preferred choice for semi-arid regions.
