Mustard

Soil Fertility:

  • Soil Types: Mustard grows best in well-drained, loamy to sandy loam soils.
  • It can tolerate some clay soils, but good drainage is essential.
  • pH: A pH range of 6.0 to 7.5 is preferred.
  • Nutrient Requirements: Requires nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, with sulfur essential for oil production.
  • Organic matter enhances soil fertility and water retention.

Temperature:

  • Climate: Mustard is primarily a Rabi (winter) crop, preferring a cool and dry climate.
  • Temperature Range: Optimal temperatures range from 10°C to 25°C.
  • It is sensitive to frost, especially during flowering.
  • Germination occurs best around 28°C.

Rainfall:

  • Moderate rainfall is required for mustard cultivation.

Mustard Varieties in India:

  • Brassica juncea (Indian mustard): The most common type.
  • Varieties are bred for high oil content, disease resistance, and regional adaptability.
  • Some common varieties include:
    • Varuna
    • Kranti
    • Pusa Bold
