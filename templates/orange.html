Orange

Soil Fertility:

  • Soil Types: Oranges thrive in well-drained loamy soils.
  • Heavy clay soils can be used if drainage is excellent.
  • Waterlogging is a major problem for orange trees.
  • pH: A pH range of 6.0 to 8.0 is generally considered optimal.
  • Nutrient Needs: Oranges require a balanced supply of macronutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium) and micronutrients (zinc, iron, manganese).
  • Organic matter is very beneficial.

Temperature:

  • Climate: Oranges grow best in tropical and subtropical climates.
  • They are susceptible to frost.
  • Temperature Range: Optimal temperatures range from 10°C to 30°C.
  • Extremes of temperature can negatively affect fruit production.
  • Rainfall: An annual rainfall of 100-120 cm is suitable for cultivation.

Orange Varieties in India:

  • Nagpur Santra: A famous mandarin variety known for its juicy and sweet flavor, grown primarily in Maharashtra.
  • Kinnow: A hybrid variety, popular in Punjab and other northern regions.
  • Coorg Santra: Grown in Karnataka, known for its bright orange color and juicy flesh.
  • Khasi Santra: Predominantly grown in the northeastern states.
  • Mosambi: A sweet orange variety, also very popular.
