Tea

Soil Fertility:

  • Acidic Soil: Tea thrives in acidic soils, with an ideal pH range of 4.5 to 5.5.
  • Well-Drained: Excellent drainage is crucial, as tea plants are sensitive to waterlogging.
  • Organic Matter: Soils rich in organic matter are essential for providing nutrients and maintaining moisture.
  • Elevation: Tea performance is excellent at elevations ranging from 1000 - 2500 meters.

Temperature:

  • Warm and Humid: Tea requires a warm and humid climate with consistent rainfall.
  • Optimal Range: The optimum temperature range for tea growth is between 20°C and 30°C.
  • Temperature Effects: Temperature influences the rate of photosynthesis, and controls dormancy and growth of the tea plants.

Tea Varieties:

  • Assam Tea: Known for its strong, malty flavor. Grown in the plains of Assam. Both Orthodox and CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl) production.
  • Darjeeling Tea: Famous for its delicate aroma and flavor. Grown in the high-altitude regions of Darjeeling. Known as the "Champagne of teas."
  • Nilgiri Tea: Grown in the Nilgiri Hills of South India. Known for its strong, aromatic flavor.
  • Clonal Varieties: Many clonal varieties are developed for high yield and quality, often developed by research institutions like the UPASI (United Planters' Association of Southern India). These clonal varieties are very important in modern tea farming.
